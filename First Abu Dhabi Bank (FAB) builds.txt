First Abu Dhabi Bank (FAB) builds its digital banking, cloud-native services, and lending operations on enterprise-grade infrastructure. Their core digital platforms depend on Temenos Core Banking for modern payments and Intellect eMACH.ai for debt management workflows.FAB relies on several third-party dependencies, API integrations, and vendor solutions across its core systems:Core Banking & Processing: Temenos Core Banking provides the cloud-native infrastructure for the bank's Shari'ah-compliant and standard regional banking operations.Debt Management: Intellect Global Consumer Banking (iGCB) powers the bank's automated debt collection features and lending platform.Digital Workflows: ServiceNow acts as a third-party dependency for IT Service Management (ITSM) and IT Operations.Document & Workflow Security: eMudhra emSigner is utilized for enterprise-wide digitalization, e-signatures, and onboarding authentication workflows.Infrastructure Monitoring: Cisco AppDynamics and Splunk provide observability for FAB's cloud-native services and on-premises migrations.Payment and Gateway IntegrationsFAB's transactional applications and digital wallet services rely on integrations with third-party payment rails, including Apple Pay, Google Pay, Samsung Pay, and payit.Trusted Channel & Investment PartnersTo extend its financial products, FAB integrates with third-party platforms such as PaisaBazaar and Finmart for card distribution. In wealth and asset management, FAB has forged collaborative partnerships with T. Rowe Price to distribute global investment strategies to regional clients
